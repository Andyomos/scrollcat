500 Cats, and That Number Is Frozen

The NFT side is now settled. 500 ScrollCats, 12 designs, six rarities, one Mythic. The mint event counter on Supra reads 500 and there is no code path left to mint another.

This matters because the cat is not a picture — it is the access key to the Arena. It gates entry, it is your fighter, it holds your progress. A fixed key supply means the door to the game has a fixed number of seats. Demand for those seats can rise. The seats cannot.

That is the scarce, immovable half of the design.

$SCAT: The Sink That Never Caps

Now the other half. $SCAT lives on an Atmos bonding curve. The play-to-graduate engine works like this: you fight in the Arena, you buy cosmetics in SUPRA, and a fixed cut of that spend automatically buys $SCAT on the curve and pushes it toward graduation.

Unlike the cats, this side has no ceiling. There is no cap on how many cosmetics get bought, how many matches get played, or how far the curve climbs. Every session is fresh demand pressure on the token. The bar reads 31% today — and it only moves one direction when the game is being played.

Fixed key. Uncapped sink. Plugged into each other.

The Asymmetry Is the Whole Point

Put the two halves together and you get a one-way pairing.

To play, you want a cat — and there are only 500, forever. To play well, you spend — and that spend feeds $SCAT toward graduation. So engagement with the game tightens the cat side AND pushes the token side at the same time. One activity, two pressures, both pointing up.

That is the asymmetry: the thing you can never make more of (cats) sits in front of the thing that grows every time the product gets used ($SCAT). Most tokens are pure inflation fighting for attention. This is a closed gate feeding an open engine.
