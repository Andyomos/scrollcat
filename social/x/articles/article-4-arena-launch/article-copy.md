Provably Fair, or It Doesn't Count

In most on-chain games, the house controls the randomness. That means the house controls who wins. We didn't want your trust — we wanted to make trust unnecessary.

Every crit, every roll in ScrollCat Arena is seeded by Supra dVRF — decentralized, verifiable randomness baked into the chain itself. Nobody can rig a fight. Not another player. Not us.

And the combat engine is deterministic and open: a match is just (your cat + your opponent + the seed + the moves). Anyone can replay it and confirm the winner. No black box. No "trust me bro."

On most chains, provably-fair randomness is an expensive bolt-on. On Supra it's native. That's not a coincidence — it's why ScrollCat lives here.
