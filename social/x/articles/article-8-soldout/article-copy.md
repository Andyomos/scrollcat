The easy money move is 10,000 NFTs and a countdown timer. We went the other way.

500 is the entire roster of the ScrollCat Arena — our NFT-gated fighting game on Supra. Every cat is a key: it gates entry, it is your fighter, it carries your XP and your seat at the table. A small, fixed set keeps the gate meaningful. It keeps holders close. It means the people in here actually own the thing, not a sliver of an endless supply.

Scarce by design, not scarce by accident. 500 was always the number. Now it is locked.

What Sold Out Does and Doesn't Mean

We will say the honest part out loud, like we always do.

No more dilution, no more "new mint dumps on the floor," no more wondering how many are really out there. From here, the only ScrollCats that change hands come from holders selling to each other.

That is the clean version of scarcity — the kind that is true because the chain says so, not because a thread told you to feel FOMO.

Supply Is Frozen. The Economy Isn't.

Here is the part most people will miss. The cats stopped growing today. The $SCAT economy did not.

ScrollCat runs a play-to-graduate engine. You walk your cat into the Arena, you fight, you buy cosmetics in SUPRA — and a fixed cut of that revenue automatically buys $SCAT on the Atmos bonding curve and pushes it toward graduation. As of today the bar reads 31% and climbs every time someone plays.

So you now have a fixed asset (the cat) feeding an open-ended sink (the token). Supply that can only shrink in circulation, meeting demand that can only grow with the game. That asymmetry is the whole point.
